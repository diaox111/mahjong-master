How to Play Mahjong Solitaire

Complete beginner's guide with rules, strategies, and FAQs

What is Mahjong Solitaire?

Mahjong Solitaire is a single-player tile-matching game played with 144 traditional Chinese mahjong tiles. Unlike traditional 4-player mahjong, Solitaire is a puzzle game where you clear all tiles from the board by matching identical pairs.

The Basic Rules

The goal is simple: remove all 144 tiles from the board. You do this by clicking two identical free tiles to make them disappear.

What is a "Free" Tile?

A tile is "free" (and can be clicked) when:

  • No tile is sitting on top of it
  • At least one side (left or right) is open

Valid Moves

You can remove two tiles if:

  • They have the same picture/symbol (e.g. two Bamboo 5 tiles)
  • Both tiles are "free" as defined above

Special case: Flower and Season tiles match within their own group. Any two flower tiles can be matched together, any two season tiles can be matched together.

The 144 Tiles Explained

Suits (108 tiles)

There are three suits, each with tiles numbered 1 through 9. Each numbered tile appears 4 times:

  • Bamboos (Bamboo) - 36 tiles (1-9, four of each)
  • Characters (Wan) - 36 tiles
  • Circles (Dots/Tong) - 36 tiles

Winds (16 tiles)

Four winds, four copies each: East, South, West, North

Dragons (12 tiles)

Three dragons, four copies each: Red Dragon (中), Green Dragon (發), White Dragon (白)

Flowers (4 tiles)

One each: Plum, Orchid, Chrysanthemum, Bamboo

Seasons (4 tiles)

One each: Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter

Step-by-Step: Your First Game

Step 1: Choose a Layout

Start with the classic Turtle layout. It's the most balanced and beginner-friendly.

Step 2: Identify Free Tiles

Look at the top layer. Find tiles with no tile on top and at least one open side.

Step 3: Match Pairs

Click two identical free tiles to remove them. The tiles disappear with a satisfying animation.

Step 4: Clear the Board

Continue matching pairs until all 144 tiles are removed. If you clear the board, you win!

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Not looking ahead - Always think 2-3 moves ahead
  • Ignoring the corners - Corner tiles get locked in early
  • Waiting to use hints - Use them liberally while learning
  • Forgetting bonus tiles - Match Flowers/Seasons early (they only match within their group)

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all Mahjong Solitaire games solvable?

Most layouts are designed to be solvable, but random shuffles can sometimes create unsolvable positions. Reputable sites (like MahjongMaster) use carefully designed layouts to ensure solvability.

How long does a game of Mahjong Solitaire take?

A typical game lasts 10-30 minutes depending on the layout and your skill level. Speed runs can finish in under 5 minutes.

Can I play Mahjong Solitaire without a timer?

Yes! Most online versions (including ours) let you play untimed for a relaxing experience.

What is the difference between Mahjong Solitaire and Mahjong?

Mahjong Solitaire is a single-player puzzle matching game. Mahjong (Traditional) is a 4-player strategy game with betting and complex rules. They're completely different games.

How do I win at Mahjong Solitaire?

Remove all 144 tiles. Strategy tips: start from the top, focus on long pairs first, save bonus tiles.

Is Mahjong Solitaire good for the brain?

Yes! Studies show Mahjong Solitaire improves pattern recognition, memory, and concentration. It's often recommended for older adults to maintain cognitive function.
